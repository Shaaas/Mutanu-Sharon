The Work Chronicles of Amina

Amina was your typical Nairobi office warrior — armed with an ID lanyard, a coffee mug that was more decorative than functional, and a smile that said “I’m fine” but really meant “if one more customer asks for a discount, I might scream.”

Episode One: The Monday Morning Showdown

Monday mornings in the office were like survival games. The printers jammed, the Wi-Fi ghosted everyone, and the boss strutted in with “urgent tasks” that were only urgent to him. Amina, clutching her cold mandazi, whispered a prayer and dove into customer complaints like a gladiator entering the colosseum.

Episode Two: The Great Lunch Hour Escape

Lunchtime was sacred. But Amina’s boss believed lunch was a “myth invented by lazy employees.” So she did what any genius would — disappeared into the washroom for “a meeting with HR” (aka inhaling pilau in secret).

Episode Three: The Drama of Deadlines

There was always that one colleague who submitted work late but still managed to shine in front of the boss. Amina? She met deadlines, triple-checked emails, and still somehow got blamed for the missing stapler. Office politics, ladies and gentlemen.

Episode Four: The Friday Night Miracle

By Friday, Amina had aged 10 years. But when the clock hit 5:01 PM, she transformed. Bag packed, lipstick reapplied, and Uber already ordered — Amina became a free woman, ready to trade office gossip for dance floors and nyama choma.

Moral of the story? The office may drain you, but with wit, resilience, and the occasional mandazi, you can survive anything. Amina did. And tomorrow, she’ll do it all over again — with style.
